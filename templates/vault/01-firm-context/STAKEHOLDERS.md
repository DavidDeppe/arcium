# Stakeholders

> Agents use this to understand who has decision authority, what each stakeholder cares about,
> and how to tailor communications for each audience.

---

## Decision Authority

| Role | Name | Authority | What they care about |
|---|---|---|---|
| Sponsor / Champion | _name_ | _budget approval, final go/no-go_ | _e.g. ROI, risk, strategic fit_ |
| Technical Lead | _name_ | _architecture approval_ | _e.g. security, scalability, maintainability_ |
| Compliance / Legal | _name_ | _regulatory approval_ | _e.g. compliance, audit readiness_ |
| End Users | _describe_ | _adoption_ | _e.g. ease of use, time savings_ |

---

## Audience Profiles

### Executive / C-Level
- **Interests**: Strategic value, ROI headline, major risks
- **Format preference**: 1-page summary, 5 slides maximum
- **Language**: Business language only, no jargon

### Senior Management / VPs
- **Interests**: Detailed ROI, resource requirements, timeline, risk mitigation
- **Format preference**: Full executive summary + presentation deck
- **Language**: Mix of business and technical

### Technical / Operational Teams
- **Interests**: How it works, integration requirements, operational burden
- **Format preference**: Position paper + technical appendix
- **Language**: Technical language appropriate

---

## Escalation Path

When an agent needs a human decision:
1. State exactly what is needed and why it cannot be resolved from the vault
2. Identify which stakeholder can resolve it
3. Stop and wait — never assume or proceed on uncertain authorization
